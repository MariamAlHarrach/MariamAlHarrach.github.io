About Me

I was born in 1991 in Lebanon. I obtained an Engineering degree in Electronics and Computer science in 2013 from the Lebanese university in Tripoli, Lebanon. Alongside the engineering degree, I completed a Master's degree in Medical Instrumentation and Technology also at the Lebanese University. Afterward, in the same year, I obtained a Labex (Laboratories of Excellence) Ph.D. grant to pursue my doctorate at the Sorbonnes universities, Université de Technologie de Compiègne (UTC) in Compiègne, France. In 2016, I defended my Ph.D. thesis about the modeling of surface electromyogram and force relationship by data analysis of a high-resolution network.

In 2017, I was a temporary assistant professor at the same university. Then, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher for two years at the LARIS Laboratory of Angers University in Angers, France. This research work consisted of the processing of MRI, fMRI, and DTI brain images for the analysis of neonatal stroke impact on the long term brain structure and characteristics. Since 2019, I am a research engineer at the National Institute of Health and Medical Research (INSERM), based at the LTSI (U1099) Laboratory in Rennes, France. My research work revolves around the computational modeling of brain networks in the context of drug-resistant epilepsy.

My main research interests consist of computational modeling, biomedical signal processing, and medical image processing. I have participated in several national, and international workshops and conferences. I am a reviewer for several journal papers such as the Journal of neural engineering (JNE), Frontiers in Neuroscience, Biomedical Signal Processing and Control (BSPC), and the IEEE Engineering in Medicine and Biology (EMBC) conference. I have also published and co-authored many international peer-reviewed journal and conference papers.
