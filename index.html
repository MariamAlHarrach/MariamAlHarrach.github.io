About Me

Mariam Al Harrach was born in 1991 in Lebanon. She obtained her Electronic and Electric engineering degree and her M.sC in Medical Instrumentation and Technology in 2013 from the Lebanese university in Tripoli, Lebanon. She obtained a Labex (Laboratories of Excellence) Ph.D grant in 2013. In 2016, she received her Ph.D degree in Biomedical engineering from the University of Technology of Compiegne in Compiegne, France. She has been a temporary assistant professor at the same university. Her research interests revolve around signal and image processing. More precisely medical images and signals analysis and computing. She has participated to several national, and international workshops and served also as a reviewer for IEEE Engineering in Medecine and Biology (EMBC) annual conference, Frontiers in Neuroscience and Biomedical Signal Processing and Control (BSPC) journal. Dr. Mariam Al Harrach has co-authored a number of international peer-reviewed journal and conference papers. Currently, she is a postdoctoral fellow with the LARIS (Laboratoire Angevins de recherche en ingénierie des systèmes) Lab at the University of Angers, Polytech Angers, France.
